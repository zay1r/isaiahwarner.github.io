Golf Dashboard

This project is a data-driven golf performance tracker built in Excel, featuring a responsive dashboard. The system analyzes my golf shot distances, accuracy, and directional trends, providing me with actionable insights to refine my game.

I created this Dashboard to:

  • Track and visualize golf shot distances across various clubs.
  • Identify accuracy trends to improve shot consistency.
  • Develop a user-friendly dashboard that dynamically adjusts to input data.
  • Leverage data analysis techniques in Excel to uncover performance patterns.

Data Collection

  • Data was collected from my golf shots over a period of time, including club type, distance, and shot direction.
  • Data was stored in an Excel spreadsheet for easy manipulation and analysis.
  • Data was cleaned to ensure accuracy and consistency.

Dashboard Design and Features

  • Created a responsive dashboard that updates in real-time as new data is entered.
  • Implemented pivot tables and charts to visualize key performance indicators (KPIs).
  • Included slicers for easy filtering of data by club type, distance, and shot direction.
  • Used conditional formatting to highlight trends and outliers in the data.
  • Descriptive Statistics: Mean, median, mode, and variance for shot patterns.
  • Comparative Insights: Club-by-club performance breakdown
  • Visual Representations: Heatmaps and scatter plots for intuitive data exploration.

Results and Key Takeaways:

The key findings within this data are the tendencies of my shot shapes It was found that for all my clubs my main shot direction was straight, with 38% of my shouts going straight 35% of my shots are going in the left direction It can be assumed that if I don't change anything with my shot there is just over a 70% chance the ball will go straight or left Furthermore when I use my irons 85% of the time my shot will go left or straight With my longer clubs such as my driver, hybrids, and fairway woods the distribution is a lot more even seeing around a 20% split of all directions

Final Thoughts

This data is essential to enhance golfing for me now that I know what clubs to use for a given distance and what shot direction I tend to hit towards concluding the findings that I may have to weaken my grip on the club to hit more shots straight In the future, I am looking to implement some analytics to compare my shot speed and distances to the PGA Tour Professionals and see the difference between our shot distances and club speed I am also looking to include a function to calculate how far the ball will go based on the club speed, ball spin rate, and other key measures. I believe this forecasting algorithm can be achieved by linear regression in tandem with python. I'm looking forward to adding to this data set and watching the change in trend throughout the coming months
